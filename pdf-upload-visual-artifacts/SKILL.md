# PDF Upload Visual Artifacts

## What this skill debugs
This skill is used to diagnose and mitigate visual anomalies that appear in PDF documents *after* they have been uploaded to SpotDraft via the **Upload & Sign** or **Third-Party Paper (TPP)** workflows. 

## When to use this skill
Use this skill when a user reports that an uploaded PDF looks correct on their local machine, but exhibits any of the following symptoms within SpotDraft:
- Unexpected **strikethroughs** on text that was not modified (e.g., after a signature is added).
- Distorted **column formatting** or spacing issues after PDF-to-Word conversion.
- **Numbering mismatches** or structural changes when viewing the document in OnlyOffice.
- Missing text, corrupted document errors during comparison, or issues with vendor stamps.

## Known Issue Patterns
SpotDraft relies on PDF processing libraries and OnlyOffice to render, convert, and stamp documents. PDFs generated by certain third-party tools (or scanned in specific ways) often contain complex, non-standard internal structures:
- Hidden layers or unflattened annotations (like vendor stamps).
- Non-standard font encodings.
- Complex nested lists that fail to parse correctly during DOCX conversion.

**Examples of this pattern:**
- **SPD-42304:** After the first user signs an Upload & Sign document, a specific clause (Section 5.2.1) suddenly appears with a strikethrough, even though no edits were made.
- **SPD-42173:** Numbering changes from `3.1 a)` to `3. 1. 2. 3.` when a TPP contract is viewed in OnlyOffice, but looks fine in MS Word.
- **SPD-34608:** Column formatting becomes distorted after PDF to Word conversion in TPP.
- **SPD-39288:** Affixed vendor stamps cause signing issues or document corruption.

## Investigation Workflow

1. **Verify the Original Document:**
   - Download the original PDF provided by the customer.
   - Open it locally in a standard PDF viewer (e.g., Adobe Acrobat, Chrome).
   - Confirm that the visual artifact does *not* exist in the original file. (If it does, the issue is with the client's source file).

2. **Identify the Trigger Point:**
   - Determine exactly when the artifact appears:
     - Immediately after upload?
     - When clicking "View Contract" (OnlyOffice rendering)?
     - After the document is converted to Word (TPP)?
     - After a signatory signs the document (stamping process)?

3. **Test the Workaround (Chrome Print):**
   - Perform the Chrome Print workaround (detailed below) on the original document.
   - Upload the new "Chrome Printed" document to an internal test workspace.
   - Replicate the exact steps (e.g., send for signing, view in OnlyOffice).
   - Verify if the artifact is resolved.

## Mitigation / Workaround

The most reliable and immediate fix for malformed or complex PDFs is the **Chrome Print Workaround**. This process flattens the PDF structure, removes hidden layers, and normalizes fonts, making it compatible with SpotDraft's processing engines.

**Instructions for the Customer:**
1. Open the original PDF file in **Google Chrome**.
2. Press `Ctrl + P` (Windows) or `Cmd + P` (Mac) to open the print dialog.
3. In the "Destination" dropdown, select **Save as PDF**.
4. Click **Save** to generate a new, flattened PDF file.
5. Create a new contract in SpotDraft using this newly saved PDF.

*Note: For Upload & Sign documents, this requires creating a new contract. The existing corrupted contract cannot be "fixed" retroactively once the artifact is baked in.*

## Escalation Triggers
Escalate to Engineering (`#pod-editor` or `#pod-signing` depending on the trigger point) if:
- The Chrome Print workaround **fails** to resolve the visual artifacts.
- The issue affects a large batch of standard templates generated by SpotDraft rather than an externally sourced/uploaded document.
- The PDF corruption completely blocks the signing flow and cannot be bypassed.
